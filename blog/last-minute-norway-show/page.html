Last minute Norway Festival!

Foreword

Every now and then I get a call that goes something like:

"Hey man I'm doing this [tour/festival] in [cool new place]. I'd need to get your flights now though, are you available?"

And whilst this is absolutely awesome, and a perfect example of the sort of thing that keeps me in love with the crazy world that is the music industry - it can be stressful. Suddenly I've went from having complete mental clarity, knowing where I'm going to be each hour of any given day of the week… to frantically calling clients, students, and friends to reschedule/source deps.

I pride myself on being able to provide consistency with everyone I work with - but sometimes career moves simply have to take priority - It sucks in this weird roundabout way, but I'm fortunate enough to work with equally busy musicians who totally understand how it goes. As for the drum students, I make sure they get a high level dep, continuing their progression path based on my notes… but as someone who was and still is a student of the percussive arts, there's nothing more reassuring than having a teacher who's actually "been there, done that" (or more appropriately "is there, doing that").

As for this specific gig, we were set to play the biggest indoor venue in Stavanger, Folken; as part of the yearly Mussikfest festival. The guys and gals who put this on are absolutely awesome.

In any case - here's my experience with Norway.

The Meat & Potatoes

*bit of a subtle nod to Scouse, a notoriously Liverpudlian dish with roots in Norwegian cuisine

Arrival (day 1)

I actually didn't really know much about the gig before setting off, only where my flight was taking me, what songs I'd be playing, and when I'd be getting home. The timeline for this particular gig was pretty packed - practically no time for any real sight seeing - arrive in Norway on a Friday, perform on the Saturday, and then get our behinds down to London for two session gigs on the Sunday.

My flight from Liverpool, UK took me to Stavanger, Norway - where myself and my close friend Jimi Boswell (guest performer for this show) met up with Falling Doves front-man Christopher Levya. We drove from Stavanger airport to our accommodation for our short stay - a lovely little AirBnB nestled within some fantastically rural surroundings. It become apparent very quickly that Stavanger was/is something of a sea-side fishing town.

On the drive up to our digs, we stopped a couple times to quickly appreciate the scenery that our short-lived place of work had to offer. Chris had already been settled in for a couple days, and must have spotted a couple things he wanted to explore at some point - so on our way we stopped off at a lakeside monument that had a really cool vibe about it:

I later came to learn through the help of some lovely locals who seemed to have enjoyed our set - that these swords were embedded into the rocks to commemorate the unification of Norway after some big battle. Pretty neat.

After our exchange with the Stavanger swords, we parked up near our accom & walked through a ridiculously pleasant park to get to the Norwegian version of Target/Tesco:

Our walk above took us to this proper whimsical park - we had a laugh and got a great nights sleep after tiring ourselves out (for real, this looked like something from Alice in Wonderland):

After spending some time taking pictures & messing around a lil, we got to heading to what I assume was a Norwegian Tesco/Target, where we got some local fruits & snacks. I personally was in favour of cooking this massive fish - but "democracy" voted it was probably a tad big for the oven…

Once our little "fishing" trip concluded, we finally got to our place of stay. The immediate surroundings were beautiful - it certainly relieved some amount of stress, accumulated from the speed at which this gig was thrown together

Home at last, Chris whipped up his famous "Norwegian delight" - which we consumed plenty of before heading to bed before show day.

Gig Day (day 2)

We started the day off with a quick breakfast, and headed straight to the venue to scope out the situation. Always best to know about any potential issues hours in advance rather than minutes… lol. Anyway - we played a nice academy sized venue named "FOLKEN" - who might I say, have some of the nicest crew I've ever worked with! I learned Norway has a fantastic musician's union - seems workers are happier when they're paid fairly, who'd have thought…

In any case, after checking on the setup, we realised we were in pretty safe hands - aside from having to sort some last minute adapters for our UK/US plugs, we didn't have any real issues. So - we had time to kill. Folken was just one of many local venues (a mixture of indoor & outdoor venues) participating in a local music festival - funnily enough this festival was occurring the same day as a massive Iron-Maiden show, so during the day every venue was absolutely rammed with Norwegian metal-heads.

After seeing some sights, shooting some pool, and chatting some of the artists we shared a stage with, it was time to get back to Folken and do what we were actually brought here for…

After playing an awesome show (made possible, largely due to the skill and professionalism of our monitor tech Anders) I was fortunate enough to be invited behind the console, getting to see the shape of another cog in the machine that is live production.

The band pictured above (mannequin leg) were just one of many fantastic local bands that really brought the heat. So glad to have made some friends on this short journey.

Home Time (Day 2.5)

Cut to about 6 hours after we've finished playing and hanging out with new friends/fans, myself and Jimi had to head back to the UK to a sold out London show (Courtyard Theatre) with some fantastic international acts for some session work.

  • FUQA

  • Summer Vapour

  • Visa Anxiety

Whilst we weren't able to get much sleep, Jimi & I took turns napping at our connecting flight in Poland. I'm not entirely sure if we got a full REM cycle in but… the London show had a positive recession. I suppose that's all that matters…

Conclusions

Rushed-ness, sleep deprivation, & big frozen fish aside - gigging in Norway was a fantastic experience. I was met with nothing but warm, sincere energy - and a genuine love & respect for touring bands.

Special thanks to Chris of the Falling Doves for contracting me for another international performance; Katie for the fantastic bassistry & meals; Mussikfest for giving us a damn good reason to get to Norway; and everybody who enjoyed our set.


Rock and/or Roll,

Jamie
